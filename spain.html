Spanish Cuisine

Spanish cuisine is a fiery celebration of bold flavors and regional diversity. From tapas shared with friends to hearty paellas by the coast, every dish is infused with passion and tradition. Savor meals in lively tavernas or bustling markets, where fresh ingredients shine.

Tapas & Mains

Enjoy Patatas Bravas, Gambas al Ajillo, or Paella Valenciana at Casa Lucio in Madrid for authentic flavors.

Regional Specialties

Try Gazpacho, a chilled tomato soup, or Fabada Asturiana, a hearty bean stew, in local eateries.

Wine & Desserts

Pair Rioja wines with Churros con Chocolate or creamy Flan at Chocolatería San Ginés.

Best Foods to Try in Spain

Paella

A saffron-infused rice dish with seafood, chicken, or vegetables.

Patatas Bravas

Crispy potatoes with spicy tomato sauce and aioli.

Jamón Ibérico

Cured ham, a Spanish delicacy with rich, nutty flavors.

Churros

Fried dough sticks served with thick hot chocolate.

Spain Weather

Spain’s diverse climate ranges from Mediterranean warmth to cooler northern regions, making it a year-round destination. Here’s what to expect:

Spring (Mar-May)

Mild and pleasant (12-22°C), perfect for city tours and festivals.

Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot and sunny (20-35°C), ideal for beaches and siestas.

Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Warm and comfortable (15-25°C), great for wine regions and hikes.

Winter (Dec-Feb)

Cool and mild (5-15°C), with sunny days in the south.

Tip: Pack light layers, sunscreen, and a hat for summer, and a jacket for northern winters.
